**Research Period:** September 2024 - March 2026 (18 months)
**Sources:** EdSurge Research, AECT Publications, Related Industry Reports

## Executive Summary

This report synthesizes findings from industry reports and research articles published within the last 18 months addressing **algorithmic bias in learning analytics** and **adaptive testing validity**. The research reveals significant concerns about equity, transparency, and validation in educational AI systems, alongside documented performance metrics from both successful implementations and reported limitations.

### 1.3 Specific Bias Concerns Documented

#### Early Warning Systems (EWS)
- **Florida School District Case (2021):** EWS data shared with police department labeled students as "future delinquency" and "destined to a life of crime"
- **Privacy Concerns:** No data source confirms whether EWS data remain private
- **Effectiveness:** EWS is effective ONLY when utilized for family and school interventions, NOT for law enforcement or disciplinary purposes

#### Algorithmic Bias in AI Grading & Assessment
- **Content Knowledge Gap:** "If you don't know your content, it's hard to tell whether or not it's accurate" - High school French teacher
- **Hallucination Risk:** AI systems generate imaginary content that appears authoritative
- **Bias in Training Data:** "When we talk about bias, we look at hiring data and incarceration data... and facial recognition systems where error rates vary depending on who the system is trying to recognize"

### 2.2 Performance Metrics: Validity Concerns

| Metric | Finding | Source | Context |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| **Cognitive Load Issues** | Students struggle with instructions, navigation, unclear affordances | ISTE+ASCD Research (2026) | Technical complexity gets in the way of learning |
| **Motivation Issues** | Kids shut down when features feel intimidating or frustrating | ISTE+ASCD Research (2026) | Affects engagement and completion rates |
| **Differential Experience** | Struggling, multilingual, reluctant readers experience products differently | ISTE+ASCD Research (2026) | Same product, vastly different user experiences |

| **Feedback Timing** | Grammar feedback during idea generation feels disruptive and demotivating | Student feedback to writing tool | Students want feedback aligned to their process stage |

### 2.3 Student-Reported Validity Issues

#### What Students Want from Edtech (ISTE+ASCD Research, 2026)
- Clean, intuitive UI tested by real students
- Clear learning progressions showing what's next
- Language and scenarios that reflect who they are
- Feedback that feels human: timely, specific, supportive, aligned to process
- Tools that respect their intelligence (reject infantilizing features)

#### What Students Reject
- Chatbots that talk too much, repeat questions, "feel like a bot"
- Excessive customization options (adults assume students want this)
- Badges and points systems
- Infantizing features
- Feedback that feels punitive rather than supportive

## Section 3: Critical Findings & Recommendations

### 3.1 Algorithmic Bias: Key Takeaways

1. **Early Warning Systems Show High False Positive Rates**
   - 80% false positive rate in Wisconsin study
   - Disproportionate impact on Black, Hispanic, and low-income students
   - No measurable improvement in graduation rates

2. **Privacy and Data Sharing Concerns**
   - EWS data shared with law enforcement in Florida case
   - No transparency on data retention and sharing practices
   - Most edtech apps share student data with third parties

3. **Teacher Skepticism on AI Assessment**
   - 25% of teachers say AI tools do more harm than good (Pew 2024)
   - Concerns about hallucinations and accuracy
   - Bias in training data affects output reliability

### 3.2 Adaptive Testing Validity: Key Takeaways

1. **Student Usability Often Overlooked**
   - Adult-centered design fails to capture student experience
   - Cognitive load and motivation issues not addressed in traditional evaluations
   - Differential experiences for struggling and multilingual learners

2. **Assessment Design Flaws**
   - Read-aloud features create anxiety rather than support
   - Feedback timing misaligned with student workflow
   - Automated grading lacks nuance for complex work

3. **Implementation Gaps**
   - Tools that impress adults in demos fall flat with students
   - Engagement drops when features feel intimidating
   - Students reject infantilizing and overly chatty AI interfaces

### 3.3 Recommendations from Sources

#### For School Leaders (ISTE+ASCD)
- Ask: Can students independently navigate the tool?
- Ask: Do multilingual learners and struggling readers experience friction?
- Ask: Does the tool maintain or diminish motivation?
- Ask: How does feedback feel to a child: supportive or punitive?

#### For Policymakers
- Distinguish between entertainment-driven and educational technology use
- Maintain local control over device and screen-time policies
- Require transparency on algorithmic decision-making in EWS

#### For Educators
- Use AI as "thought companion" not replacement for expertise
- Vet tools for security and privacy (FERPA, COPPA compliance)
- Focus on AI literacy: teaching students how to prompt and fact-check
