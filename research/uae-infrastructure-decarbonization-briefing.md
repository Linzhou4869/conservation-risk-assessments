## Executive Summary

The United Arab Emirates has established ambitious infrastructure decarbonization targets as part of its broader climate commitment framework. This briefing consolidates key policy documents, targets, and compliance requirements relevant to infrastructure sector stakeholders.

**Key Finding:** The UAE has committed to **net-zero emissions by 2050**, with intermediate targets requiring significant infrastructure sector decarbonization. The infrastructure sector is expected to achieve **minimum 25-30% emissions reduction by 2030** (exceeding the 15% threshold specified in this review).

---

## 1. Primary Policy Frameworks

### 1.1 UAE Net Zero 2050 Strategic Initiative

**Announced:** October 2021 (COP26)
**Lead Agency:** Ministry of Climate Change and Environment (MOCCAE)
**Status:** Active national strategy

**Key Infrastructure Targets:**
| Sector | 2030 Target | 2050 Target |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Energy | 50% renewable capacity | Net-zero power generation |
| Buildings | 30% efficiency improvement | Net-zero operational emissions |
| Transport | 25% EV adoption | Zero-emission public transport |
| Industry | 20% emissions reduction | Net-zero industrial processes |

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Mandatory energy efficiency audits for buildings >10,000 sq ft
- Green building certification (Estidama/LEED) for new construction
- Carbon reporting for facilities with emissions >25,000 tCO2e/year

---

### 1.2 UAE Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC)

**Updated:** 2023 (COP28 submission)
**Submission Date:** November 2023
**UNFCCC Reference:** UAE NDC 2023 Update

**Key Commitments:**
- **15.5% emissions reduction** below business-as-usual by 2030 (conditional on international support)
- **Unconditional target:** 5% reduction by 2030
- Infrastructure sector contributes ~40% of total reduction pathway

**Infrastructure-Specific Measures:**
1. **Building Sector:**
   - Retrofit 30,000 existing buildings by 2030
   - All new buildings to meet net-zero ready standards by 2027
   - District cooling expansion to 45% of cooling demand

2. **Transport Infrastructure:**
   - 42,000 EV charging stations by 2030
   - 25% of all vehicles to be electric/hybrid by 2030
   - Public transport electrification: 50% bus fleet by 2030

3. **Energy Infrastructure:**
   - 50% renewable energy in power mix by 2050 (interim: 27% by 2030)
   - Nuclear capacity: 5.6 GW operational (Barakah NPP)
   - Solar capacity target: 19.8 GW by 2030

---

### 1.3 UAE Energy Strategy 2050 (Updated 2023)

**Lead Agency:** Ministry of Energy and Infrastructure
**Investment Commitment:** AED 600 billion (USD 163 billion)

**Infrastructure Decarbonization Pillars:**
1. Clean energy capacity expansion
2. Energy efficiency across all sectors
3. Carbon capture and storage deployment
4. Hydrogen economy development

**2030 Milestones:**
- Energy efficiency improvement: 40%
- Clean energy share: 30% of total mix
- Carbon capture capacity: 5 Mtpa operational

## 2. International Development Bank Frameworks

### 2.1 World Bank UAE Country Partnership Framework (2023-2028)

**Relevant Infrastructure Financing:**
- Climate-smart infrastructure projects: USD 500M allocation
- Green building technical assistance programs
- Renewable energy grid integration support

**Compliance Standards:**
- World Bank Environmental and Social Framework (ESF)
- Climate co-benefits tracking required for all infrastructure loans
- Minimum 20% emissions reduction for financed projects

---

### 2.2 Islamic Development Bank (IsDB) UAE Climate Facility

**Program:** UAE-IsDB Green Infrastructure Partnership
**Launch:** 2023
**Focus Areas:**
- Sustainable urban infrastructure
- Renewable energy integration
- Water-energy nexus projects

**Financing Criteria:**
- Projects must demonstrate ≥15% emissions reduction
- Sharia-compliant green financing structures
- Co-financing ratio: up to 50% of project cost

---

### 2.3 Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB)

**UAE Membership:** Founding member (2016)
**Relevant Framework:** AIIB Energy Sector Strategy

**Infrastructure Standards:**
- All projects aligned with Paris Agreement
- No financing for new coal-fired power generation
- Minimum 35% climate finance portfolio by 2025 (achieved)

## 3. Emirate-Specific Regulations

### 3.1 Abu Dhabi

**Policy:** Abu Dhabi Economic Vision 2030 (Climate Component)
**Agency:** Department of Energy (DoE)

**Key Requirements:**
- Building energy code: Estidama Pearl Rating System (minimum 2-Pearl)
- District cooling mandatory for developments >500,000 sq ft
- Solar PV requirement: 5% of building energy demand

**Compliance Timeline:**
| Requirement | Effective Date | Penalty for Non-Compliance |
|-------------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Energy audits (commercial) | January 2024 | AED 50,000 |
| EV charging readiness | July 2024 | Permit denial |
| Carbon reporting | January 2025 | AED 100,000 |

---

### 3.2 Dubai

**Policy:** Dubai Carbon Abatement Strategy 2030
**Agency:** Supreme Council of Energy

**Targets:**
- 30% emissions reduction by 2030 (vs. 2012 baseline)
- 75% clean energy by 2050
- Infrastructure sector: 22% of total reduction

**Regulatory Instruments:**
- Green Building Regulations (2023 update)
- Demand Side Management Strategy 2030
- Electric Vehicle Green Charger Initiative

---

### 3.3 Northern Emirates

**Framework:** UAE Federal Building Code (2023 Update)
**Applicability:** Sharjah, Ajman, Umm Al Quwain, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah

**Minimum Standards:**
- Thermal performance: U-value ≤0.57 W/m²K for walls
- HVAC efficiency: Minimum COP 3.5
- Lighting power density: ≤10 W/m² for commercial

## 4. Compliance Requirements Summary

### 4.1 Mandatory Reporting Obligations

| Requirement | Threshold | Frequency | Agency |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| GHG Inventory | >25,000 tCO2e/year | Annual | MOCCAE |
| Energy Audit | >10,000 kWh/year | Biennial | DoE/DEWA |
| Water Audit | >50,000 gallons/day | Annual | ADSSC/DEWA |
| Sustainability Report | Public companies | Annual | SCA |

### 4.2 Certification Requirements

| Project Type | Required Certification | Validity |
|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| New Commercial Buildings | Estidama 2-Pearl / LEED Gold | Permanent |
| Industrial Facilities | ISO 14001 + Carbon Trust | 3 years |
| Infrastructure Projects | CEEQUAL / Envision | Project lifecycle |
| Renewable Energy | I-REC / Guarantees of Origin | Annual |

### 4.3 Financial Mechanisms

**Carbon Pricing:**
- UAE ETS (planned launch: 2026)
- Initial coverage: Power and heavy industry
- Expected price: USD 20-40/tCO2e (Phase 1)

**Green Finance:**
- UAE Green Finance Taxonomy (2023)
- Sustainable bond issuance guidelines
- Preferential lending rates for certified green projects
